Risk Guide

<75°F wet bulb: generally manageable.
75–80°F: strenuous activity feels much harder.
80–85°F: high heat illness risk during exertion.
85°F+: dangerous; aggressive cooling and schedule changes matter.

How It Works

Wet bulb is calculated using the Stull approximation from temperature and relative humidity. Dew point entries are converted to relative humidity first. Forecast mode uses hourly NWS temperature plus dew point or humidity when available.

Forecast data may lag, fail, or omit humidity in some grids. Treat results as guidance, not medical advice. Hydration, shade, acclimatization, sun exposure, medications, and activity level all matter.
