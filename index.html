How to read the numbers

Dew point is usually the best quick humidity number. Above 70°F feels oppressive.

Wet bulb combines heat and humidity. Around 80°F is very taxing for strenuous outdoor activity.

Heat index estimates how hot it feels in shade. Direct sun can feel substantially worse.

  • Wet bulb under 75°F: generally manageable
  • 75–80°F: uncomfortable; use breaks and water
  • 80–85°F: high heat-stress risk with exertion
  • 85°F+: dangerous; avoid strenuous activity
