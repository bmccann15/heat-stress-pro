Wet-Bulb Risk Guide

<75°F: usually manageable.
75–80°F: hard exertion gets much tougher.
80–85°F: high risk during sports/work; cool aggressively.
85–88°F: dangerous, especially for long exposure.
88°F+: extreme danger; postpone strenuous outdoor activity where possible.

Important Limits

This is guidance, not medical advice. Sun, wind, clothing, hydration, acclimatization, age, medications, illness, and exertion can change risk dramatically. Wet bulb is not the same as Heat Index.

Data

Forecast mode uses public National Weather Service hourly forecast grids and a free ZIP-code geocoder. It works for U.S. locations. If NWS omits humidity/dew point for a grid hour, that hour may be skipped.
